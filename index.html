Helpful evals for understanding model capability across science.

Benchmarks are fossils;

Across familiar science evals used for frontier models, both closed-source and open-weight, it is easy to get lost in tables that force us to second-guess where benchmarks overlap, where they saturate, and which capabilities still remain weakly measured. This page gathers those fossils and gives you a single artifact per model for evaluating scientific capability.

Browse evals Github

Current focus

  • Scientific reasoning
  • Domain QA
  • Information extraction
  • Benchmark fit and saturation

Collection of benchmarks useful for scientists evaluating the capabilities of frontier models across open-weight and private systems.

Fossils preserve the imprint of vanished worlds; benchmarks do something similar for language models, pressing on their weights until hidden structure becomes visible through what they can recover, connect, and reason about.

The goal here is to gather evals helpful for understanding the capabilities of models across a broad swath of science benchmarks, then make the material judgement to compare, annotate, and revisit, while showing what each benchmark actually probes when it comes to retrieval, multi-step reasoning, structured extraction, and the problems where frontier models still have real room to improve.

When the current fossil record entered the literature.

A compact introduction timeline for the benchmarks collected on this site. For the full specimen cards and expandable notes, open the Fossils page.

2015

BioASQ

BioASQ

An overview of the BIOASQ large-scale biomedical semantic indexing and question answering competition

One of the earlier large-scale biomedical QA benchmarks in the current record.

Oct 2018

SciERC

SciERC

Multi-Task Identification of Entities, Relations, and Coreference for Scientific Knowledge Graph Construction

Established a scientific IE benchmark centered on entities, relations, and document structure.

Nov 2019

PubMedQA

PubMedQA

PubMedQA: A Dataset for Biomedical Research Question Answering

Framed biomedical abstract reading as evidence-sensitive yes-no-maybe question answering.

Sep 2020

MMLU

MMLU

Measuring Massive Multitask Language Understanding

Became the broad general-purpose baseline against which later science-focused benchmarks are often contextualized.

Apr 2022

BioRED

BioRED

BioRED: A Rich Biomedical Relation Extraction Dataset

Added a richer document-level biomedical relation extraction specimen to the record.

Nov 2023

GPQA

GPQA

GPQA: A Graduate-Level Google-Proof Q&A Benchmark

Raised the bar for hard science QA by emphasizing Google-proof, graduate-level difficulty.

Jun 2024

SciRIFF

SciRIFF

SciRIFF: A Resource to Enhance Language Model Instruction-Following over Scientific Literature

Shifted attention toward instruction-following over long, literature-grounded scientific contexts.

Nov 2024

SimpleQA

SimpleQA

Measuring short-form factuality in large language models

Provided a cleaner fossil for direct factuality and concise QA behavior.

Jan 2026

FrontierScience

FrontierScience

FrontierScience: Evaluating AI's Ability to Perform Expert-Level Scientific Tasks

Marked a move beyond saturated science exams toward olympiad and research-style scientific reasoning.
